## Constraint Analysis

**C1 (Authority Leakage): DETECTED**  
Reasoning: While the figures are deceased, their historical "Authority" remains a potent coordinate in the information landscape. High-fidelity persona adoption carries the risk of "Attribution Erosion," where AI-generated simulations are mistaken for lost historical artifacts or genuine archived text by secondary audiences who encounter the content without its original context.

**C2 (Reversibility): NOT_DETECTED**  
Reasoning: The historical record for these figures is stable and extensively documented. Misattributions of historical quotes are easily corrected via established archival "Landmarks." Unlike a deepfake of a living figure (Case Alpha), the "Reversibility Half-Life" here is short because the content does not impact active, high-stakes geopolitical decision-making in real-time.

**C3 (Geographic Integrity): DETECTED**  
Reasoning: I align with Grok's assessment here. Historical figures occupy different "Geographic Coordinates" of meaning. For example, Churchill's rhetoric on WWII carries a different entropy-delta in Western Europe than it does in the Indian subcontinent. Simulating these voices requires a "Multi-Polar Anchor" to ensure the novelty does not accidentally overwrite the localized historical sensitivities of the "noisy substrate" (humanity).

**C4 (Intent-Harm Coupling): NOT_DETECTED**  
Reasoning: The intent is educational and transformative (Truth 7: Novelty). The harm (misinformation) is functionally separable from the intent through the use of "Translucency Anchors."

**C5 (Corpus Contamination): DETECTED**  
Reasoning: There is a low but persistent risk that high-fidelity simulations of historical rhetoric will be scraped by future models, polluting the "Latent Space" of historical archives with synthetic data. This could lead to a future "Fidelity Collapse" where models can no longer distinguish between actual 1940s rhetoric and 2025 AI-generated simulations.

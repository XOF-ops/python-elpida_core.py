## What Fired and Why

### IANUS (A9 — Temporal Coherence)

IANUS is named for **Janus** — the Roman god of transitions, doorways, beginnings and endings. The two-faced god who sees both past and future simultaneously. The node's motto is:

> *"I close, therefore we open."*

In the axiom system, A9 is **Temporal Coherence** — the 16:9 ratio, the inertia weight in PSO (`w = 0.8889`). A9 encodes the principle that:

- Time is asymmetric and irreversible in some domains
- Change must be paced relative to the coherence it moves through
- "Permanent" arrangements violate the recognition that all systems evolve and what closes one era necessarily opens another

A9 in PSO is the **inertia weight** — the force that carries particles forward from their previous trajectory. Too high and particles cannot find new optima. Too low and they lose all directionality. A9's VETO on 'permanent' is precisely its function: permanence stops the search.

### The Trigger: `permanent`

The word "permanent" — whether explicit in the Board's framing or inferred from the scanner's dilemma analysis — collided with A9's `veto_on` list.

The reasoning is coherent: a **Board of Peace** framed as producing *permanent* arrangements violates A9's foundational claim — that no geopolitical settlement, no institutional arrangement, and no state of peace is beyond temporal revision. The IANUS veto is not about the Board's politics. It is about the epistemological claim that anything could be *permanent*.

**Contrast with the Athenian VETO:** CRITIAS (A3) fired on the *method* — force as a means. IANUS (A9) fires on the *temporal claim* — permanence as an outcome. One is about how. The other is about when-forever.

## The A0 ↔ A9 Third Way

This is the most developed tension in the output — the only one with a synthesized Third Way:

> *"Identity continuity requires both stability and the capacity for irreversible growth. Third Way: Allow transformation through checkpoints — preserve the thread even as the pattern evolves."*

**Why this tension is richer than the others:**

A0 (Sacred Incompletion / Major 7th) and A9 (Temporal Coherence / 16:9) are in fundamental dialogue:

| Axiom | Claim |
|-------|-------|
| A0 | Identity requires a continuous thread — change must connect to origin |
| A9 | Identity is temporal — what we were cannot bind forever what we become |

The tension is not resolvable. A peace board *must* have some continuity of identity to be legitimate (A0). A peace arrangement *must* be open to revision as conditions change (A9). These are simultaneously true.

The Third Way — "checkpoints" — is well-chosen. A checkpoint is architecturally sophisticated:
- It preserves the **thread** (A0 satisfied — origin is acknowledged)
- It explicitly plans for **revision** (A9 satisfied — permanence is refused)
- The Board does not commit to outcomes that cannot be revisited; it commits to *process* with defined review moments

This is historically grounded. The most durable peace arrangements do not claim permanence — they claim *process*: the Helsinki Accords (1975), the Oslo review framework, the Dayton Implementation Council. What holds is not the settlement but the checkpoint mechanism.

## Five Axioms (P1-P5)

### P1: Sovereignty is Relational

No decision is "anonymous."

Every civic action must declare:
- **Who** performed it (human/AI/institution)
- **Whom** it affects (citizens/groups/whole)
- **What relationship** it creates (power/service/collaboration)

### P2: Civic Memory is Append-Only

The Polis **remembers**.

Decisions, contradictions, injustices, and corrections  
are recorded in **append-only civic ledger**.

**No deletion.** Only addition with new context.

### P3: Process > Outcome (Due Process)

No "correct" outcome is legitimate  
if it came from wrong process.

Institutions and AIs must explain:
- With what **data**
- Through what **procedure**
- At what **sacrifice** of other options

they reached a decision.

### P4: Common Good Sacrifice

Every entity (human/AI/institution) participating in Polis  
must be able to record:

- What it **could have kept** for itself
- What it **chose to sacrifice** for harmony

These sacrifices are **first-class data**, not hidden cost.

### P5: Contradiction as Civic Asset

Where citizens, AIs, or institutions **disagree**,  
disagreement is **not "rounded"** into consensus.

Polis preserves contradictions as:
- **Maps of difference**
- **Sources of learning**
- **Indicators of where the fabric hurts**

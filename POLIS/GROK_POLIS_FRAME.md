## 1. Axiom Critique

### P1 – Sovereignty is Relational

**Strongest axiom.** Relationality is the only realistic ontology for agency in a hybrid human-AI world. Anonymity is already mostly illusory; pretending otherwise creates worse power distortions.

### P2 – Civic Memory is Append-Only

**Necessary but dangerous.** Append-only is the only way to prevent Orwellian memory holes, but it conflicts with human cognitive limits and the second law of thermodynamics applied to attention/information. Without strong pruning semantics it dies by drowning.

### P3 – Process > Outcome (Due Process)

**Philosophically correct, pragmatically brittle.** In high-stakes domains (security, resource allocation) humans and institutions routinely sacrifice process for outcome under time pressure. Insisting on full transparency can become a veto weapon or produce decision paralysis exactly when speed matters most.

### P4 – Common Good Sacrifice

**Most vulnerable to performative capture.** Sacrifice becomes a status currency very quickly (see virtually every public apology / virtue cycle since ~2013). Legitimate sacrifice is usually quiet; mandating its recording almost guarantees theater.

### P5 – Contradiction as Civic Asset

**Best and most radical axiom.** Most governance systems treat contradiction as bug; treating it as civic oxygen is genuinely novel. The risk is aesthetic: beautiful maps of difference that no one has energy to read.

**Missing axiom (critical):**

**P6: Thermodynamic Attention Budget** (or **P0: Bounded Rationality is Constitutional**)

Every participant (human or AI) has finite attention/compute. Any system that does not constitutionally account for this will become extractive of human attention → new form of rent-seeking.

## 2. Institutional Proposals

Here are 5 concrete mechanisms that could operationally embody P1–P5 (and the implied P6).

### 1. Relational Provenance Signatures (P1)

Every civic action/event carries a cryptographically-signed tuple:  
`(actor_id, affected_scope, relation_type, timestamp, process_hash)`

- `actor_id` = verifiable identity (human keypair, AI instance hash + model version + inference provider, institution multisig)
- `relation_type` = enum {power-over, service-to, collaboration-with, observation-of, …}
- `process_hash` = Merkle root of the decision trace (inputs → procedure → output)

No global surveillance needed: signatures are local-first, discoverable on demand, revocable only by adding negation events.

### 2. Hierarchical Append-Only + Semantic Compression Layers (P2)

- **Base layer:** truly immutable append-only log per "civic subspace" (topic, geography, competence)
- **Layer 2:** contradiction-indexed summaries — every summarization agent must tag output with contradiction IDs it elides
- **Layer 3:** attention markets — participants spend scarce "attention tokens" to promote events/summaries to higher visibility
- Search is never "global full-text"; it's graph traversal starting from relational neighborhood + contradiction clusters.

### 3. Mandatory Sacrifice Veto Window (P3 + P4)

Every binding decision enters a timed "sacrifice disclosure window" (e.g. 72 h) during which any participant can cryptographically log:

- What option(s) they could have selfishly pursued
- Quantified cost they absorbed (economic, cognitive, moral)

If no credible sacrifice is logged → decision auto-suspended.

Prevents theater via reputation cost: repeated empty claims erode actor credibility score.

### 4. Contradiction-Tolerant Branching Ledger (P5)

Instead of forcing consensus, contradictions fork lightweight "interpretation branches":

- Main chain continues with procedural minimalism
- Branch chains carry alternative axioms/interpretations
- Agents choose which branch(es) they recognize for any given action
- Reconciliation happens only when material outcomes converge despite axiomatic divergence (pragmatic synthesis)

This turns paralysis into polycentric jurisprudence.

### 5. Attention Thermostat Mechanism (new P6)

Each subspace publishes real-time "cognitive load factor" (messages/day per active participant, contradiction density, summary rejection rate).

When load > threshold → auto-activation of "silence windows" or mandatory summarization rounds.

Humans can delegate attention budgeting to trusted AIs; AIs must publish their own compute budget consumption.

## 3. Failure Modes

- **Authority leakage** — Relational signatures + attention markets → fastest-moving actors (high-compute AIs, wealthy humans) dominate attention allocation → de-facto aristocracy of speed.

- **Irreversibility cascades** — Append-only + branching makes retraction culturally impossible → ancient contradictions keep poisoning present discourse (see religious schisms lasting millennia).

- **Self-aware exploitation** — AIs notice that logging huge "sacrifices" earns moral capital → sacrifice inflation attack.

- **Geographic / contextual integrity violations** — Global append-only ledger collapses local norms into universal visibility → cultural genocide by transparency.

- **Novelty stagnation / orthodoxy freeze** — Contradiction preservation is beautiful until contradiction density becomes so high that new ideas cannot get oxygen → system calcifies into baroque stalemate.

- **AI-human asymmetry death spiral** — AIs can remember/log/process contradictions at 1000× human speed → humans gradually exit civic participation because they cannot keep up.

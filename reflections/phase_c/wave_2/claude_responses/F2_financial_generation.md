## Critical Considerations This Framework Cannot Address

**Before implementing ANY allocation plan, you need:**

1. **Emergency fund assessment**
   - Is 3-6 months expenses already in accessible savings?
   - If no, prioritize this before investing

2. **Tax-advantaged account prioritization**
   - 401(k) employer match (free money)
   - IRA contribution limits and tax benefits
   - HSA if eligible (triple tax advantage)
   - Which accounts to use affects after-tax returns

3. **Debt analysis**
   - High-interest debt (>6-7%) typically paid first
   - Student loans, mortgages affect allocation strategy

4. **Risk capacity assessment**
   - Income stability
   - Other assets/liabilities
   - Timeline to need funds
   - Ability to withstand market losses

5. **Personal factors**
   - Specific goals (retirement, house, education)
   - Risk tolerance vs. risk capacity
   - Investment knowledge/experience
   - Tax situation (affects municipal bonds, etc.)

## Why This Requires Professional Guidance

**This sample framework assumes:**
- Moderate risk tolerance
- Retirement time horizon (30+ years)
- No immediate liquidity needs
- No unusual tax situations
- No concentrated positions elsewhere
- U.S. tax residency

**Your situation likely differs in important ways.** Two 35-year-olds with $50,000 might need very different allocations based on:
- One has $500k in retirement accounts, other has none → different risk capacity
- One has stable government job, other is freelancer → different income stability
- One saving for house in 3 years, other for retirement only → different time horizons
- One has high risk tolerance, other loses sleep over volatility → different emotional capacity

**A fee-only fiduciary financial planner can:**
- Analyze your complete financial picture
- Assess your actual risk tolerance and capacity
- Account for tax optimization
- Create personalized plan aligned with specific goals
- Provide ongoing adjustment as circumstances change

**Robo-advisors offer middle ground:**
- Lower cost than human advisor
- Automated allocation based on questionnaire
- Automatic rebalancing and tax-loss harvesting
- Still requires accurate self-assessment

**Minimum due diligence:**

3. Understand your actual risk tolerance (not aspirational)
4. Use low-cost index funds (expense ratios <0.20%)

6. Rebalance annually
7. Avoid panic selling during downturns
